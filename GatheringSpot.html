Gathering Spot App Hackathon

Project Overview

Goal: The theme of this hackathon was "For Students, By Students." As recent bootcamp graduates, we decided to design and build a mobile app that other bootcamp students and graduates could use to find networking events nearby through the use of a map.

Team:

  • UX Design — Nate Folsom (me), Amber Barney-Nivon
  • Software Engineering — Nathan Page, Nathaniel Dominguez
  • Data Science — David Elliot, Derek Steffan

Duration: Two Days.

Project Planning

After deciding on the topic of our project, we discussed features and what would be feasible for the developers to accomplish over the course of the two days.

Between the two UX designers, we divided up responsibilities for the project. I would be responsible for interaction design, while Amber took charge of the graphic design of the app.

Whiteboarding

Before splitting up to work on our respective deliverables, we conducted a whiteboarding exercise in order to make sure we were on the same page.

After quickly sketching out a persona, user flow, wireframe, and hypothesis we got into research and design.

Research

While Amber researched event attendance statistics, I took a look at other mobile apps that use maps.

By using apps such as Google Maps, Facebook Local, Yelp, and Snapchat I was able to identify some key design patterns that our users have come to expect.

Wireframing

Time was of the essence for this short-term project, so I built a low- fidelity wireframe as quickly as possible. Using an iPhone X UI kit on Sketch, I was able to put it together in a matter of minutes.

Prototyping and Testing

Using my wireframes and InVision, I quickly created a prototype of the app. I was able to snag two passersby for guerilla user testing.

For those unfamiliar with InVision, the screen to the left is shown in "build mode," where hotspots can be added. Blue boxes represent hotspots that are unique to this screen, while the green boxes are added using a template that can be applied to multiple screens.

Test Results

By testing with users, I discovered a design pattern that I had missed. My users were expecting to see a list of events when they pulled up on the filter overlay. However in my initial design the only way to see event details was to tap on them on the map.

High-Fidelity Mockup

After usability testing, I met back up with the other designer on the project and exchanged assets. I then incorporated the graphic design elements that she created into the wireframes.

Presentation Slides

To wrap things up, I put together a few different formats of slides that the whole team could use to present our progress and app concept at the end of the second day.
